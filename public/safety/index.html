Core safety rules

  • Do not share your full legal name, exact address, school, workplace, daily route, passwords, verification codes, or financial details.
  • Do not send money, cryptocurrency, gift cards, or account access to someone you meet through random chat.
  • Do not install software, open unknown downloads, or allow remote control of your device.
  • End conversations involving threats, hate, sexual harassment, coercion, blackmail, or pressure to move to another platform.
  • Assume another person could record the conversation using tools outside TalkAir's control.

Safety for minors

TalkAir is not designed for children under 18. Minors should not use anonymous stranger-chat services. Parents and guardians should use device-level controls and discuss online grooming, financial scams, and requests for private images.

Never engage in sexual communication with a minor or request images, contact information, or private meetings. Suspected child exploitation should be reported to the appropriate national child-protection or law-enforcement service.

Recognize common manipulation

Urgency

A stranger claims you must act immediately, send money, or provide a code before there is time to verify the story.

Authority impersonation

Someone claims to represent a bank, employer, government agency, platform, or law-enforcement body and demands confidential information.

Rapid emotional pressure

A person attempts to create intense trust, romance, fear, or guilt before requesting money, images, secrecy, or off-platform contact.

Technical access

Someone asks you to install a program, share your screen, run terminal commands, or reveal an API key.

Reporting and emergencies

Use TalkAir's Report control to leave the match and send an anonymous session-level report. The MVP does not record call audio, so reports cannot include a recording made by TalkAir.

If you believe there is an immediate threat to life or physical safety, contact your local emergency service. For fraud, exploitation, or serious illegal conduct, preserve relevant information you lawfully possess and report it through the appropriate official authority in your country.

What TalkAir can and cannot guarantee

TalkAir can provide controls, minimize collected information, and design the service around direct audio connections. It cannot verify every anonymous user's identity, prevent external recording, guarantee that every statement is true, or eliminate all abusive behavior. Your decisions and boundaries remain an important layer of safety.

Frequently asked questions

Does TalkAir verify user identities?

No. The current service is anonymous and does not verify identity, age, qualifications, country, or background.

Can TalkAir prevent someone from recording me?

No. TalkAir does not intentionally record calls, but another user may use external hardware or software.

What happens when I report someone?

The current interface submits a session-level report and moves you away from that match.

Should children use TalkAir?

No. TalkAir is not designed for children under 18.
