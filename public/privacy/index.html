TalkAir privacy policy

This policy describes the current TalkAir MVP hosted through GitHub Pages and Cloudflare Workers. It should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel before commercial or large-scale launch.

Scope and operator

This policy applies to the TalkAir website at https://usmnashd1.github.io/TalkAir/ and its associated signaling Worker. TalkAir is currently an independently operated MVP. A formal business identity, jurisdictional address, and dedicated privacy contact must be added before production launch.

Information processed

Microphone audio

Your browser captures microphone audio after you grant permission. WebRTC attempts to transmit that audio directly to the matched peer. TalkAir does not intentionally record or store call audio on its signaling Worker.

Signaling and session data

The Worker processes temporary session identifiers, matchmaking mode, selected topic, WebRTC session descriptions, and ICE candidates to establish a connection. Active queue and room state is ephemeral.

Chat content

Text chat is relayed through the Worker to the current peer. The application does not intentionally persist chat history after the session, although infrastructure logs or recipient actions may create copies outside the application's intended behavior.

Optional analytics

If a valid Google Analytics 4 Measurement ID is configured, TalkAir asks for analytics consent before loading Google's analytics script. If accepted, page visits and limited interaction events such as mode selections or start-button clicks may be measured. The implementation disables Google Signals and advertising personalization signals by default. You may decline, and no Google Analytics script will load from TalkAir's analytics module.

Technical data

GitHub and Cloudflare may process IP addresses, request metadata, security signals, and operational logs according to their own services and policies.

Purposes and legal basis

Information is processed to provide matchmaking, establish calls, relay chat, prevent abuse, diagnose failures, and secure the service. Applicable legal bases depend on the operator's jurisdiction and the user's location. Consent is requested by the browser for microphone access.

Sharing and international processing

Audio is shared with the person you are matched with. Signaling and chat pass through Cloudflare infrastructure. Static files are delivered through GitHub Pages. These providers may process data in multiple countries. Do not use TalkAir to communicate information requiring regulated confidentiality.

Retention and security

The application is designed around ephemeral sessions and does not intentionally maintain user accounts, call recordings, or persistent room histories. Provider logs may have separate retention periods. WebRTC media is encrypted in transit, but no internet service can promise absolute security.

Your choices and rights

You can deny microphone permission, mute the microphone, leave a match, close the site, clear browser data, and avoid submitting personal information. Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights concerning access, deletion, correction, objection, restriction, portability, or complaint to a regulator. A verified privacy contact process must be established before full launch.

Children and policy changes

TalkAir is not intended for children under 18. The policy may change as features, providers, and legal requirements evolve. Material updates should be published with a revised effective date.

Frequently asked questions

Does TalkAir record calls?

The current application does not intentionally record call audio.

Is my IP address completely hidden?

No guarantee is made. Hosting providers process network addresses, and some WebRTC configurations may expose network-related information needed for connectivity.

Are chat messages stored?

The app is designed to relay chat ephemerally rather than maintain chat history, but infrastructure logs and recipients may create copies.

How can I delete my account?

The current MVP has no registered accounts. You can close the site and clear local browser permissions or data.
