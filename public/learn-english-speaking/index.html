Why live conversation matters

Reading and listening build vocabulary and comprehension, but speaking requires a separate skill: retrieving words quickly while another person is waiting. Real-time conversation trains this retrieval, pronunciation, turn-taking, and the ability to repair misunderstandings.

You do not need perfect grammar before speaking. A useful goal is understandable communication, followed by gradual improvements in accuracy and range.

A 20-minute English practice plan

Warm up for three minutes

Introduce your learning goal and describe your day using simple sentences.

Discuss one topic for ten minutes

Choose education, technology, travel, culture, health habits, or a recent personal achievement.

Request limited feedback

Ask your partner to correct only repeated mistakes so the conversation continues naturally.

Review for five minutes

Write down useful phrases and one pronunciation or grammar point to practice later.

Conversation prompts by level

Beginner

  • Describe your hometown and your favorite place there.
  • Explain your normal morning routine.
  • Talk about food you like and how it is prepared.

Intermediate

  • Should university education be free?
  • How has technology changed communication?
  • What makes a city comfortable to live in?

Advanced

  • Does remote work strengthen or weaken communities?
  • How should societies balance privacy and public safety?
  • Which education reforms would create the greatest benefit?

How to give useful corrections

Do not interrupt every sentence. Agree on a feedback style first. A partner can repeat the corrected phrase naturally, collect two or three errors for the end, or focus on one target such as past tense or word stress.

Remember: Random partners may make mistakes too. Check uncertain advice using a reliable dictionary, grammar reference, teacher, or official exam resource.

Frequently asked questions

Can I learn English speaking for free on TalkAir?

TalkAir provides free voice matching for practice. It is a practice tool rather than a complete language course.

Do I need to be fluent?

No. Tell your partner your approximate level and ask them to speak at a comfortable speed.

Will TalkAir correct my grammar?

The current MVP does not provide automated correction. Your partner may offer feedback, but important guidance should be verified.

How often should I practice?

Short, frequent sessions are usually easier to sustain than rare long sessions. Even 15 to 20 minutes several times a week can provide useful speaking practice.
